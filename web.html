FLOWERS

Flowers are the reproductive part of flowering plants. They produce seeds, which eventually become other flowers. The primary purpose of a flower is reproduction.

In all plants, a flower is usually its most colourful part. We say the plant flowers, is flowering or is in flower when this colourful part begins to grow bigger and open out.

There are many different kinds of flowers in different areas in the world. Even in the coldest places, for example the Arctic, flowers can grow during a few months.

Flowers are made up of four whorls:-

Calyx and corolla: The two outer whorls.

Androecium : The male part of the flower.

Gynoecium : The female part of the flower.

There are so many different species of flowers in the world no one knows an exact number, but the closest estimate is somewhere between 250,000 and 400,000 species.

Roses are the most popular flowers in the UK, with around 200-300 million roses grown for Valentine's Day alone.

For the United Kingdom, red roses are the national emblem of England, thistles are Scotland's and for Wales it's daffodils.
Japan calls it's country's language of flowers, Hanakotoba.
